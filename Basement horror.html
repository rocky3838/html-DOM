The Basement

The house had been silent for hours, except for the occasional groan of aging wood and the distant rumble of thunder outside. I hadn’t meant to go down there—not tonight. But something felt off. The air upstairs had grown heavy, like the walls were holding their breath. I gripped the flashlight tighter, its plastic casing slick in my sweaty palm, and stepped onto the basement stairs. Each creaking board beneath me sounded louder than the last, echoing into the shadows below. The deeper I went, the colder it got. Not just chilly—unnatural. Like the kind of cold that clings to your bones and whispers secrets you don’t want to hear.I was actually looking for my younger brother, Aarav, who had been playing hide and seek with me earlier. The beam of light danced across the cluttered basement—old furniture, broken boxes, a rusted bicycle. I called out softly, “Aarav?” My voice barely made it past my lips. No answer.
